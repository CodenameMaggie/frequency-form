## üí° PRO TIPS

### **Finding Manufacturers:**

**Alibaba.com:**
- Search: "organic cotton t-shirt manufacturer"
- Filter: Gold suppliers, Trade Assurance
- MOQ: Look for 100-500 unit minimums
- Request samples before bulk order

**Global Sources:**
- Higher quality than Alibaba
- Better for natural fibers
- More expensive but worth it

**Direct:**
- Email brands from your original marketplace plan
- Ask if they do wholesale/white label
- Many will say yes!

### **Pricing Formula:**

```
Manufacturer cost: $20
Your wholesale price: $35-40 (75-100% markup)
Retailer sells for: $70-80 (100% markup)
Win-win-win!
```

### **Negotiation Tips:**

**With Manufacturers:**
- Start small (100-500 units)
- Build relationship
- Get better pricing as volume increases
- Lock in annual contracts

**With Retailers:**
- Offer samples first
- Start with small test order
- Set up Net 30 payment terms (they pay 30 days after delivery)
- Offer early payment discount (2% off if paid in 10 days)

### **Closing Big Accounts (Winners, TJMaxx):**

1. **Research their vendor requirements:**
   - Visit their vendor portal
   - Understand their process
   - Prepare required documents

2. **Get a warm intro if possible:**
   - LinkedIn connections
   - Trade shows
   - Industry contacts

3. **Start with one product:**
   - Don't overwhelm with full catalog
   - Pick one hero SKU
   - Prove you can deliver

4. **Be patient:**
   - Can take 6-12 months to close
   - But one PO = $100K-1M
   - Worth the wait!

## üö® COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### **1. Don't Try to Be Everything**
- Start with 2-3 product categories
- Master them before expanding
- Quality over quantity

### **2. Don't Skimp on Samples**
- Always order samples first
- Test quality yourself
- Send to retailers before bulk orders

### **3. Don't Overpromise**
- Be realistic on timelines
- Better to under-promise, over-deliver
- Build trust first, then scale

### **4. Don't Ignore Small Accounts**
- 50 small accounts = 1 big account
- Small accounts close faster
- Use them to refine your process

### **5. Don't Forget Follow-ups**
- Most deals close on 3rd-5th touch
- Set up CRM reminders
- Be persistent but not annoying
